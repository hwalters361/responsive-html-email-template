Harvey Mudd Nelson Speaker Series: Jaron Lanier, Sept. 19

Description: First part in 3-part series delving into the ethical, social, aesthetic and technological dimensions that shape our lives in the emerging AI age. Admission to this public lecture series is complimentary.

"There is No AI" While he is at the very center of AI developments, Jaron Lanier also has a radically different take on AI. He doesn’t think AI is a thing in itself but is instead a new kind of social collaboration. AI as we know it today combines the expressions of real humans in new and useful ways. A chatbot borrows from things real people have said before and recombines them, for instance. This perspective opens up more useful ways to think than the usual science fiction framing, which treats the programs as mysterious, potentially scary creatures. Instead of using hard-to-define terms like “safety” or “fairness” to improve AI, we can ask whose input was important to a given output. That concreteness suggests ways to spread both lines of responsibility and opportunity. Instead of asking who will be put out of work by AI, we can ask who should be incentivized and rewarded for offering better data to go into AI programs. Lanier is also one of the few scientists working in the field who is good at explaining how the programs work to non-technical audiences.

Book signing & reception at 5:15 pm, talk begins at 6! The lecture will be held in the auditorium of the R. Michael Shanahan Center for Teaching and Learning at Harvey Mudd College.
